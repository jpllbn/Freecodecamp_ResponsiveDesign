Ginataang Kalabasa

Ginataang Kalabasa, a Filipino vegetable dish, consists of squash, string beans, and coconut milk. While it can be a vegetarian dish, it often includes shrimp, pork, or chicken for added flavor. Ginataang Kalabasa traces its origins to the Philippines, where coconut milk has been a mainstay in cooking due to the country’s wealth of coconut trees.

Ingredients

  • 3 lbs. kalabasa cubed
  • 18 pieces string beans cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 ½ lbs. pork belly diced
  • 24 ounces coconut milk
  • 2 tablespoon shrimp paste
  • 1 onion chopped
  • 3 thumbs ginger julienned
  • 8 cloves garlic
  • 8 grams Maggi Magic Sarap
  • 1 cup water

Instructions

  1. Heat the wok and then sear the pork until enough fat renders. Move the pork aside, and sauté garlic, onion, and ginger in the pork fat. 1 onion, 3 thumbs ginger, 8 cloves garlic, 1 ½ lbs. pork belly
  2. Once the onion softens, pour the coconut milk. Cover and simmer for 40 to 50 minutes. 24 ounces coconut milk
  3. Add shrimp paste and water. Let boil. 2 tablespoon shrimp paste, 1 cup water
  4. Add the kalabasa. Cover the wok. Cook for 8 minutes. 3 lbs. kalabasa
  5. Put the string beans in. Stir. Continue cooking for 5 minutes. 18 pieces string beans
  6. Season with Maggi Magic Sarap. 8 grams Maggi Magic Sarap
  7. Serve. Share and enjoy!
